# Introduction

Fabric Studies connects textile science with fashion design practice. It helps students understand why one fabric falls softly, another holds a crisp line, why some garments must be cut on lengthwise grain, and why fibre choice changes comfort, care and performance.

<div class="callout-band" markdown>
  <strong>Core idea:</strong> A fabric is not only a surface. It is a structure made from fibres or yarns, and that structure decides behaviour.
</div>

## What students will learn

  </section>
</div>

## Fabric study lens

When examining a fabric swatch, use four questions:

| Lens | Ask this | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Fibre | Is it natural, regenerated or synthetic? | Predicts comfort, absorbency, warmth, care and sustainability. |
| Yarn and structure | Is it woven, knitted or bonded? | Predicts stretch, stability, drape and durability. |
| Direction | Where are warp, weft, selvage and bias? | Controls garment hang, fit and cutting accuracy. |
| Finish and use | How does the surface behave? | Guides end use: apparel, home textiles, medical, industrial or performance products. |

## Course map

1. Fundamentals of Fabrics builds the vocabulary: grain, warp, weft, selvage, bias, seam, dart, pleat and other garment details.
2. Methods of Fabric Formation explains weaving, knitting and bonding.
3. Natural Fabrics studies cotton, wool and silk.
4. Synthetic Fabrics studies rayon, nylon, polyester and acrylic.
5. Interactive Quiz gives a changing revision set each time students access the website.
